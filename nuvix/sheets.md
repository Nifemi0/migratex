\# 1. Brand Core



\## Brand Name



\*\*Nuvixes Studio\*\*



\## Domain



\*\*nuvixes.studio\*\*



\## Brand Essence



Nuvixes Studio creates websites that make businesses look credible, refined, and worth trusting.



The brand is not meant to feel flashy, trendy, loud, or temporary. It should feel composed, modern, and deliberate. Every brand decision should reinforce seriousness, trust, clarity, and visual maturity.



\## Brand Promise



Clients should feel that working with Nuvixes Studio gives them a website that looks intentional, trustworthy, and visually clear.



\## Brand Mission



To help businesses and startups present themselves online with structure, clarity, and credibility.



\## Brand Function



Nuvixes Studio is a premium web design studio focused on creating websites that make brands look established, serious, and reliable.

\# 5. Creative Direction



\## Core Creative Direction



\*\*80% editorial\*\*

\*\*20% brutalist tension\*\*



This is the defining visual formula of the brand.



\## What Editorial Means Here



Editorial gives the brand:



\* whitespace

\* hierarchy

\* calm confidence

\* visual restraint

\* typography-led layouts

\* premium composition

\* quiet sophistication



\## What Brutalist Tension Means Here



Brutalist influence gives the brand:



\* sharper typography moments

\* occasional bold scale shifts

\* slight layout tension

\* confident contrast

\* more edge, less softness



\## What This Does Not Mean



This does not mean:



\* harsh black-and-white posters

\* chaotic asymmetry everywhere

\* messy layouts

\* ugly-on-purpose design

\* random experimental visuals



The brutalist layer should be subtle. It should create tension and confidence, not disorder.



\## Overall Look



The brand should feel like:



\* a modern premium studio

\* a creative business with taste

\* a structured practice with point of view



It should not feel like:



\* a SaaS dashboard brand

\* a dev template

\* a crypto landing page

\* a “look at all these animations” site

\# 7. Logo Usage Rules



\## Correct Use



\* Maintain original proportions

\* Use only approved color variants

\* Use high contrast against the background

\* Preserve consistent spacing around the logo

\* Use the primary lockup where brand presence matters

\* Use icon-only when space is limited



\## Incorrect Use



\* Do not stretch the logo

\* Do not rotate the logo

\* Do not skew or distort it

\* Do not add glow

\* Do not add gradients

\* Do not add shadows

\* Do not change stroke thickness inconsistently

\* Do not recolor it with random colors

\* Do not place it on busy, noisy, or low-contrast backgrounds

\* Do not crowd it with text or UI elements



\## Brand Principle



The logo should always feel:



\* quiet

\* clear

\* intentional

\* premium

\* nav links

\* Light background → `rgba(30,32,48,0.08)`

\* Soft border on light bg → `rgba(30, 32, 48, 0.10)`

\* Soft border on dark bg → `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.10)`

\* Subtle text on light bg → `rgba(30, 32, 48, 0.70)`

\* Subtle text on dark bg → `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.70)`



\## Non-Negotiable Rule



\*\*No extra colors. Ever.\*\*



No cyan.

No purple.

No random bright blue.

No gradients used as a substitute for hierarchy.

\# 13. Typography System



\## Primary Typeface



\*\*Neue Montreal\*\*



This is the official brand typeface.



\## Why It Works



Neue Montreal works because it feels:



\* editorial without looking old

\* modern without looking generic

\* structured without becoming sterile

\* slightly sharp without becoming harsh



It pairs well with the logo and supports both the editorial base and the subtle brutalist tension.



\## Font Pairing Recommendation



Official recommendation:



\* \*\*Neue Montreal only\*\*



This keeps the system:



\* cleaner

\* more premium

\* easier to control

\* less likely to become inconsistent



A second font should not be introduced unless there is a strong practical reason later.



\## Approved Weights



Use a restrained set:



\* \*\*500\*\* for headings

\* \*\*400\*\* for body

\* \*\*300\*\* for subtle support text only if needed



Avoid weight overload.

\# 18. Layout and Grid System



\## Core Principle



The layout should feel:



\* quiet

\* intentional

\* spacious

\* structured

\* premium



\## Grid System



\### Desktop



\* \*\*12-column grid\*\*



\### Tablet



\* \*\*8-column grid\*\*



\### Mobile



\* \*\*4-column grid\*\*



\## Container Width



\* Max width → \*\*1200px\*\*



\## Text Width



\* Preferred text width → \*\*760px to 840px\*\*



This keeps the brand feeling editorial instead of stretched and generic.



\## Layout Rhythm



Use:



\* large clean margins

\* fewer, stronger elements

\* clear section separation

\* controlled vertical rhythm



Do not cram too much into one section.



\## Brutalist Tension in Layout



Use lightly.



Good examples:



\* one oversized headline

\* one left-shifted block

\* one asymmetrical moment

\* one section with stronger alignment contrast



Do this once or twice per page, not everywhere.
